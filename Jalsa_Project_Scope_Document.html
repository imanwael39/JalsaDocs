Project Scope Document — AI-Powered Mental Health Clinic Management System

Document Version: 1.0 Date: June 20, 2026 ITI Capstone 2026 Status: Approved for Planning

1. Project Information

AttributeDescription
Project NameJalsa (جلسة) — AI-Powered Mental Health Clinic Management System
Project TypeGraduation Capstone Project (ITI 2026) — Full-stack web application with integrated AI services
Industry DomainHealthcare — Mental Health / Clinical Psychology — Arabic-language clinic operations
Project SummaryJalsa is a web-based clinic management platform that digitizes patient intake, session documentation, therapeutic exercise assignment, progress analytics, AI-assisted referral report generation, and patient support chat with crisis detection. The system serves therapists and patients through role-based access, backed by Azure OpenAI services and a RAG pipeline for contextual clinical decision support.
Business ProblemArabic mental health clinics rely heavily on manual paperwork, unstructured session notes, and time-consuming referral report preparation. Therapists lack consolidated visibility into patient progress across assessments, sessions, and exercises. Patients have limited between-session support channels, and crisis signals in digital communications may go undetected. Data fragmentation reduces care continuity and increases administrative burden.
Proposed SolutionA unified clinic management system with: (1) digital intake and OCR-based form extraction, (2) session notes with voice-to-text transcription and AI summarization, (3) exercise assignment and patient self-tracking, (4) real-time AI support chat with crisis escalation, (5) RAG-powered referral report generation with therapist review workflow, and (6) analytics dashboards for clinical progress monitoring — all delivered as a responsive web application deployable on Azure.

2. Project Vision

Vision Statement

To become the leading Arabic-language, AI-augmented mental health clinic platform that empowers therapists with intelligent clinical tools while giving patients safe, continuous support between sessions.

Mission Statement

Deliver a secure, user-centered clinic management system that reduces administrative overhead, improves documentation quality, and enhances patient safety through responsible AI integration — built by a balanced full-stack team as an ITI Capstone demonstration of real-world software engineering practice.

Expected Impact

  • Reduce therapist time spent on referral report drafting by automating draft generation with human-in-the-loop approval
  • Improve intake efficiency through OCR and digital form completion
  • Enable data-driven treatment monitoring via assessment trends and exercise analytics
  • Increase patient engagement through assigned exercises and AI-supported chat
  • Enhance patient safety via automated crisis keyword detection and therapist alerting

Long-Term Goals

  • Extend platform to multi-clinic operations and native mobile applications
  • Integrate with hospital EMR systems and wearable health devices
  • Support additional Arabic dialects and standardized psychological assessment instruments
  • Advance AI models with clinic-specific fine-tuning and expanded RAG knowledge bases

6. Project Scope (In Scope)

All features below are traceable to the 52 use cases defined in the UML Use Case Diagram and organized into the modules requested for this scope document.

6.1 Authentication & Authorization

Description: Secure identity management for therapists and patients with role-based access control.

Main Features: Register Therapist Account; Login/Logout (Therapist & Patient); Reset Password; Manage Profile; JWT token management; password hashing.

Related Actors: Therapist, Patient

Related Use Cases: UC-A01 through UC-A07 (7 use cases)

Expected Outputs: Authenticated sessions, secure API access tokens, password reset emails, updated user profiles.

6.2 Patient Management

Description: Full lifecycle management of patient records including intake and archival.

Main Features: Create/Update/View Patient Profile; Search Patients; Archive Patient; Digital Intake Form; Upload Intake Form Image (OCR).

Related Actors: Therapist, Patient, AI Services

Related Use Cases: UC-P01 through UC-P08 (8 use cases)

Expected Outputs: Structured patient records, searchable patient lists, archived patient status, completed intake data.

6.3 Therapist Management

Description: Therapist account and profile management within the authentication domain (no separate multi-therapist admin module in MVP).

Main Features: Therapist registration; therapist login/logout; profile management; therapist-facing dashboards and chat monitoring.

Related Actors: Therapist

Related Use Cases: Register Therapist Account, Login (Therapist), Logout, Manage Profile, View Chat Engagement Summary, Receive Crisis Alerts

Expected Outputs: Registered therapist accounts, authenticated therapist sessions, profile updates, crisis notification delivery.

6.4 Assessment Management

Expected Outputs: Assessment records in database, trend charts on progress dashboard, auto-populated assessment fields from intake OCR.

6.5 Session Management

Main Features: Create/Edit Session Note; View Session History; Search Session Notes; Record Voice Memo; View AI Session Summary.

Related Actors: Therapist, AI Services

6.6 Exercise & Treatment Management

Description: Therapeutic exercise assignment, tracking, and completion monitoring.

Main Features: Assign/Update/Deactivate Exercise; Extend Due Date; Monitor Completion; View Assigned Exercises; Mark Completed/Partially Done/Skipped; Submit Reflection Notes; Exercise Recommendation Assistant (AI).

Related Actors: Therapist, Patient, Notification Service

Related Use Cases: UC-E01 through UC-E10 (10 use cases)

Expected Outputs: Exercise assignments, status updates, completion analytics, exercise reminder notifications, patient reflection notes.

6.7 Notification Management

Related Actors: Therapist, Patient, Notification Service, Email Service, AI Services

6.8 AI Services

Description: Core AI infrastructure supporting all intelligent features across the platform.

Main Features: OCR intake extraction; Voice STT (Whisper); Embeddings & RAG; Context retrieval; Patient history summarization; Report draft generation; Support chatbot responses; Conversation memory; Crisis keyword NLP detection.

Related Actors: AI Services, Therapist, Patient

Main Features: Generate/Review/Edit/Approve/Reject Referral Report; Export PDF; View Previous Reports; Patient Progress Dashboard; Assessment Trends; Exercise Analytics; Session Statistics; Chat Engagement Summary; Admin/System Analytics.

11. Assumptions

  1. Azure OpenAI service (GPT-4o, Whisper, Vision) will be provisioned and accessible throughout development.
  2. Team has access to Azure cloud resources for deployment (App Service, SQL, Redis).
  3. A single mental health clinic operates the system (no multi-tenant requirements in MVP).
  4. Therapists are responsible for validating all AI-generated clinical content before use.
  5. Patients have internet access and modern web browsers supporting audio recording APIs.
  6. Email delivery service (SendGrid or equivalent) is available for password reset workflows.
  7. Project supervisor availability for periodic reviews during the 4–8 week development period.
  8. UML Use Case Diagram v1.0 represents the authoritative functional baseline (52 use cases).
  9. Arabic-language content and clinic workflows are primary; English may be used in technical documentation.
  10. AI crisis detection supplements but does not replace professional clinical judgment.
  11. Team of 5 developers remains intact for the full project duration.
  12. Academic evaluation focuses on MVP completeness rather than commercial-scale performance.

19. Scope Summary

What the project will deliver: Jalsa — a production-ready, AI-augmented mental health clinic management web application covering 52 business use cases across authentication, patient management, sessions, exercises, chat, AI reports, analytics, and admin monitoring — deployed on Azure.

Who will use it: Mental health therapists (primary users) and their patients (secondary users), supported by AI services and limited admin monitoring capabilities.

What problems it solves: Eliminates manual intake paperwork via OCR, automates referral report drafting with therapist oversight, centralizes patient progress data, enables between-session AI-supported chat with crisis detection, and provides analytics for data-informed care decisions.

What is intentionally excluded: Native mobile apps, payment processing, video conferencing, multi-tenant SaaS, hospital EMR integration, wearable devices, full admin user management, and AI model fine-tuning.
